Cimol

Cimol is a typical Sundanese (West Javanese) snack made from starch (aci) shaped into balls and then fried. It is famous for its chewy texture on the inside and crispy on the outside, and is often served with various savory powdered seasonings such as spicy, cheese, or BBQ. The name "cimol" comes from the Sundanese word, aci digemol, which means starch flour shaped into balls

Ingredients

  • Tapioca flour (Aci)
  • Water
  • Seasoning

Steps

  1. Heat the water until very hot (this is important to prevent the aci from bursting when fried)
  2. Prepare the tapioca flour and mix it with the seasoning
  3. Gradually pour the hot water into the flour while mixing
  4. Shape the dough into small balls by hand
  5. Prepare oil in a pan, add the aci balls, and fry over low heat until cooked
  6. Ready to be served!
