Seblak

Seblak is a traditional street snack from West Java (Bandung), known for its spicy and savory flavor. It is made from softened crackers cooked with aromatic spices such as kencur (aromatic ginger), garlic, and chili, and combined with various toppings like eggs, sausages, meatballs, or chicken feet. Seblak can be served either soupy or dry.

Ingredients

  • Crackers
  • White & green onion
  • Chilli
  • Aromatic ginger (kencur)
  • Oil
  • Eggs
  • Seasoning
  • Water

Steps

  1. Slice the green onion into pieces
  2. Crush the chilli, white onion, and aromatic ginger into a paste
  3. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the spice paste until fragrant
  4. Add water (just enough to soak the crackers) and crack in the eggs, then scramble
  5. When the water starts to simmer and the eggs are cooked, add the crackers and green onion
  6. Cook until the crackers soften and absorb the spices
  7. Ready to be served!
